Get set up
▼
Step 1 — Open Copilot
Go to m365.cloud.microsoft/chat on your laptop. Sign in with your Rocket IT Microsoft 365 account.
Step 2 — Open App Builder
In the left sidebar, open "Agents" and choose "App Builder". If you don't see it, look in the Agent Store for "App Builder" and add it. Then come back to this page and start with the first prompt.
Step 3 — You're ready
That's it. Type or paste a prompt into App Builder. Watch the app appear and keep refining it in the same conversation. To iterate: say what you want changed and App Builder updates the app. To start fresh: open a new App Builder conversation.
Secondary option — Google AI Studio
If App Builder is not available, go to aistudio.google.com, sign in with a Google account, and click "Build" in the left sidebar. Then use the same prompts below.
Tip — If something breaks
Copy any error message and paste it back into the chat. Say "fix this." Seriously — that's it. If the conversation gets messy, start a new chat with a better prompt. Fresh start beats patching.
Exercise 1
What's in your fridge?
▼

Make sure you're in App Builder in Copilot. Copy each prompt, paste it in, and see what happens.

Prompt 1 — The simple ask
I have chicken thighs, rice, a lemon, garlic, and some wilted spinach. What should I make for dinner?
Prompt 2 — Add your real life
Actually, here's my real situation: - I have about [YOUR TIME] to cook - I can't eat [YOUR RESTRICTIONS] - I'm cooking for [NUMBER] people - I'm in the mood for something [VIBE — comforting? light? impressive?] - Skill level: [be honest] - Goal: [just tonight / meal prep for the week / something that makes great leftovers] Give me 3 options ranked by effort. Include timing for each step so I know what to start first. If I pick a meal-prep option, tell me how to batch it and what keeps well.
Prompt 3 — Now make it build
Stop giving me text. Build me an interactive app instead. I want to: - Check off ingredients I have from a list - Set my dietary restrictions and preferences - Choose a mode: just tonight / meal prep for the week / great leftovers - Choose a vibe: quick & easy, comfort food, impress someone - It generates recipe cards with cook time, difficulty rating, step-by-step instructions, and a photo description - If I pick meal prep, show a weekly plan and a combined shopping list for anything I'm missing Make it visually beautiful. Make it feel like a real cooking app.
Bonus — Push it further
Try adding any of these to your app: "Only suggest recipes from SeriousEats.com and NYT Cooking — sources I actually trust." "Remember my preferences: I hate cilantro, I'm dairy-free, and I always have sriracha. Use that every time I come back." "Add a recipe converter — let me switch between metric and imperial." "Add a 'use it before it expires' mode where I flag ingredients by freshness."
Capstone
Your challenge
▼
The challenge
Find something in the world you want to make 10x better. Build it as an interactive app. RULES: • JOYSTICKS — the user clicks, types, drags, or controls something (not just reads) • MOAT — what makes yours special? • You'll present it with a pitch deck at the end You just built a cooking app from a fridge check. Now go bigger.
Exercise 2
Make it real
▼

Apply the same stair-step to your capstone project.

Prompt 4 — First shot
Build me a [your project idea in one sentence].
Prompt 5 — The real brief
Open a new conversation. Answer these in your own words, all in one message: WHO is this for? (a real person, not "users") WHAT can they DO? (3-5 real interactions) WHY does it matter? (what's broken today?) WHAT SHOULD IT LOOK LIKE? (style, layout, vibe) WHAT SHOULD IT NOT DO? (no login screens, no fake data, no features you didn't ask for) End with: "Build this as a complete interactive app."
Prompt 6 — Iteration tips
Fix it: • "The layout should be [specific change]" • "The button does X but should do Y" • "This section is confusing — simplify it" Push it: • "Add [specific feature]" • "Make it work on mobile" • "Add animations when [action] happens" If the conversation gets messy — start a fresh chat. Paste your brief with improvements. Fresh start beats patching.
Exercise 3
Ship it — slides
▼

One person on slides. Other two polish the app. Divide and conquer.

Prompt 7 — Pitch deck
Create a pitch deck as a single interactive HTML presentation with clean, visual slides. THE PROBLEM: [what's broken — one sentence] OUR APP: [what we built — one sentence] HOW IT WORKS: [3 things the user can do] WHY IT'S SPECIAL: [your moat] WHAT WE LEARNED: [one surprise from building this] 5 slides. Big fonts. Minimal text. Make it look professional.
Takeaway
Lines to remember
▼

Key ideas from today. Come back to this page anytime!

The mental model shift
It's not a search bar. It's not a person. It's an agent. You brief it, you direct it, you review its work, you send it back.
The skill
The people who get the most out of AI aren't the most technical. They're the ones who know their own situation the best. That's you.
When you're stuck
You don't need to know how to get there. You need to know how to describe where you want to go. The agent figures out the path.
When it breaks
Paste the error back in and say "fix this." That's not failure — that's iteration.
Over-prompting
If you already told it your situation, don't repeat yourself. It heard you. Just say what you need next.
Take this home
Everything you did today — the exercises, the prompts, the format — you can run this same workshop with a client or another group. You have everything you need. It's on your phone.
